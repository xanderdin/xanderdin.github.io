What if I reinvent the "wheel"?!

2018-11-17
Translations: RU

Olga, my wife is a professional artist and an interior designer. She’s using brushes, paints and pencils in a perfect way. But when it comes to a computer the matters become a little bit worse. Nevertheless, she mastered a couple of graphical applications and even such a big “monster” for 3D modeling as Blender!

But for the interior design neighther drawing a picture nor even building a 3D model is quite enough. Builders require technical drawings. But my wife is having much troubles when it comes to CAD applications. So, she was drawing technical drawings with a regular pencil on regular paper sheets.

Meanwhile, I lost my job and was having strong professional burnout that kept me in troubles when I was trying to find another one. I felt like changing my profession is what I really needed. At that time we decided that I would try to take a part in my wife’s designing job and start drawing technical drawings.

No sooner said than done. Step by step, I started doing that new for me occupation. Being an old fan of free open source software, there’s no wonder why I started using exactly that software and in this field. By the way, one of the main tools I’m using in this field is LibreCAD. One, second project, yet another… Gradually, the full picture started to become visible and the clear understanding of how to organize my workflow. Appeared my own templates, my components for drawings in my library became growing in number.

At some point I realized that there is a lot of repetitive manual work which was stealing a lot of my time, and that has to be automated. And then I felt like now was the time to apply my former professional knowledge.

The “wheel” named enumsheets

One of the final stages of making drawings is numbering the sheets, counting the total number of sheets and making the contents. The matter is when initially drawing those sheets the final number of them is yet unknown. Also the final numbers may change by adding/removing sheets or by changing their ordering. When those numbers are already known, each sheet must be reopened once again and those numbers must be inserted into its title block fields. Also you have to copy each sheet title to your contents page. A usual design project consists of 50-60 drawings sheets. Opening each of them and setting those values requires a couple of hours of boring manual work. Imagine now if a mistake was made somewhere in the beginning or middle of those sheets! Or if again you need to add or remove or reorder them. Why should we do all that manual job if we have computers?!

That was the reason why enumsheets was made. The script counts the total number of sheets, searches for title block in each of them, updates required values including sheet number, total number of sheets, and optionally sets date, scale and address. Also the script extracts the titles of each sheet and saves them as contents in an Excel file. And all that in seconds.

The script is mainly targeted to our process of preparing drawings as it’s required for my wife’s design work (http://artidea.gallery). But as far as all main parameters of the script are configurable it could be adjusted and for needs of someone else. Hope, it would ease someone’s job also.

So, sheets are ready, what else?

Sure, there are yet many things require automation. For example, when all sheets are prepared, they still are stored each in its own dxf file. But we also need to prepare them for printing. In our case we need one big pdf file with all the sheets in it. And what, should we again open each sheet and print it to a single pdf file? Sure not, although initially that was the case. And I’m going to tell how I automated that stage in my next article. But for now, I wish you good luck in reinventing your own “wheels” :) !
