My Pocket Tomtit

2019-04-13
Translations: RU

I bet nobody of previous or current employees of “Integral” company is able to explain with absolute confidence until now, why “See All” mobile application in Google Play has my.pocket.tomtit ID instead of something like ua.integral.seeall. But having straight relation to the app, I’m going to tell some words about its roots.

Let me start with the moment when I was just an ordinary sysadmin in a computer club. I was usually giving to my servers and workstations vivid names, not just weird personless srv10, ws06, etc. And because I was living in a small coastal town, I was usually giving names correlating to marine environment. I had servers such as Beluga, Lobster, Seaweed, Dogfish, Pigfish, Sawfish, Skate, Swordfish, Turtle, Jellyfish, Jetty, Submarine, Flounder, Octopus. The last one was called so because it had eight modems connected to it. I was using the same practice when naming my own personal projects which were not meant to be shown to the crowds.

Back in 2008 or even earlier, in “Integral” company, where I was working for a long time, there was an idea of creation of an online service allowing users of mobile devices to be able to control remotely their own home burglar alarm panels. But because that idea never had a priority, and also because of absence of someone who could implement it, it continued to be just an idea. Time at a time there were some efforts towards its progress, but always more important tasks appeared and the idea was again put into drawer.

In 2014 there was a period when my work tasks were primarily of a routine nature and no any continuous development process were done. I was long dreaming about trying myself in mobile development and decided the time for that had come.

I started learning Android development, and as far as the subject is pretty deep, dived into it with my whole head. When understanding came of Android applications’ possibilities and restrictions, decided to try implementing another concept project for “See All”. But before beginning making mobile app, the backend was required.

Basic functionality of “See All” required possibility for users themselves connect their burglar alarm panels using Internet, and by using the app be able to arm/disarm and continuously monitor their one or multiple premises.

For delivery of messages to mobile app I refused using standard Push-notifications because first, I was against dependencies on Google, especially when Google, according to US sanctions, started blocking its services to the known region of Ukraine. Second, I was planning implementation of the same app on other mobile platforms, and wanted something own and consistent with all future platforms.

My previous work experience with Jabber helped me choose XMPP protocol as main transport, sufficient enough for the needs of “See All” and available almost for all popular platforms. Moreover, it allowed using, monitoring and managing of burglar alarm devices by utilizing a simple set of commands sent by means of any existing Jabber client from any existing main platforms, including mobile devices and computers. And also there were already existing implementations of XMPP servers, by choosing any of them one could get immediately ready to use solution for managing users, security polices, and other features, eliminating the need to program and debug all that from scratch.

So, for XMPP server I chose OpenFire. Now it only left to design database structure and to create a plug-in for OpenFire, which had to run with OpenFire and served as a server for burglar alarm devices, receiving their events and saving those events to database table. For the role of database server I chose the one well recommended itself, when I was using it in other projects, PostgreSQL, with its events trigger system, suited perfectly to the requirements. Everything is simple – an event from a burglar alarm device arrives, the plug-in writes it to database table, a trigger fires code converting the received event in another format suitable for sending to a monitoring client, saves that message to another table, and OpenFire sends the message from that table to required recipients. Everything is pretty simple and works like a charm.

The XMPP protocol was extended so as to allow users of ordinary Jabber messengers receive and send simple text commands, but for the mobile app data was transmitted in a structured way in XML. Because the XMPP protocol is build upon XML, so in order to eliminate one more instance of parsers I decided to use XML for extended data also, although later I began thinking JSON could be more convenient, universal and a better choice.

I named that project Tomtit. Why Tomtit? At that time I was working with Java and I had running Tomcat application server. Also at that time Twitter was a popular way among nations for sending short messages. Marine theme already bored me and I decided switching to sky environment. While looking for birds names, Tomtit got into my view, and it correlated perfectly with those sitting in my head Tomcat and Twitter. Such a word salad.

When the backend was ready I started developing Android mobile app. Dived into it completely. Worked on the project when spare time was available both at work and home. The implementation of the app was going rather hard to me. Sometimes I felt real pain when my head was almost ready to burst because of continuous thinking and huge amount of data.

Nevertheless, step by step the app became forming its shape. I wasn’t confident of final result, therefore nobody was aware of the project I was working on so hard in my spare minutes. The app was absolutely of an experimental nature, that was aimed first to my acquaintance with the new field of knowledge and understanding of what I should or shouldn’t await of such an app.

As it is known, every Android application has to get its unique ID which is set usually at project creation, and later its change, as stated in documentation, is problematic. Having creator’s domain name as part of that ID is a common practice. Because of not having my personal domain name, I had to think about something different.

As I already mentioned, the backend project had name Tomtit. My phone, being first target for the app, was usually borne in my pockets. And that’s the root of that simple ID – my.pocket.tomtit.

Also was thinking of another variant, my.hand.tomtit, which was based on Russian proverb “A tomtit in your hand is better than a crane in the sky”, which pictured me some associations with my app. Nevertheless, my.pocket.tomtit sounded more rhythmic to my ear and finally the decision was made completely in its favor.

I even managed to draw an emblem for the project :)

When the experimental app began getting a complete shape I understood that the prototype was successful and it could become the “See All” project. Because the app was not published in Google Play yet I thought about changing its ID such as to include parts of domain name of company I was working for. But at that period of time, because of the known events in Ukraine, there wasn’t firm confidence for the destiny of integral.ua domain. Another domain name in .ru zone was planned, also seeall.modi and вижу.рф domains were registered, but their destiny was also questioned. So my decision was not to change the ID, but continue using all as it already was.

New emblem borrowed from openclipart.

Time to make an announcement of the app had come and in the beginning of 2015 the app was published in Google Play and Yandex.Store. After that I continued working on it officially. To the moment of my last release it had several improvements and several errors were fixed. It was translated into English, Russian and Ukrainian, had many pre-installed melodies for events and ability to set one’s own. The app allowed registering a new user, new burglar alarm devices, configure, manage and continually monitor such devices. As a bonus, there was a possibility of exchanging of short messages between users sharing a single burglar alarm panel.

  

  

Besides the app for “See All”, a little bit later I created yet another Android app – Integral-SOS, which was able to get and display in convenient way notifications from burglar alarm panels without using Internet, independently from servers, getting events by SMS. Certainly, that another app had lesser functionality compared to “See All”, but nevertheless, had a potential for its own niche without competing with “See All”. For that app I chose ID related to integral.ua (ua.integral.sos.app) from the very beginning. But, as it turned out, nobody from my executive stuff was interested in that another app at all and it continued absolutely unneeded.

Next I was experimenting with hybrid versions of “See All” app that when written once would be able running on multiple mobile platforms. In order to accomplish that I dived into JavaScript, Angular, Ionic, Meteor and other related worlds.

Hybrid app, Variant 1

  

Hybrid app, Variant 2

  

But that turned out to be the wrong direction, because of many issues with different versions of web-engines, and also difficulties in implementation of continuous monitoring by such a hybrid app. Also, about a year after my first announcement, very few efforts were made in order to market the “See All” service and the number of users for the past year not increased. That killed any desire to work on it anymore.

After my leaving the company it seems nobody touched my.pocket.tomtit app anymore. Fifth year is passing by while it continues to stay in Google Play without change. Despite the fact that I rated it as an alpha-version it was working relatively stable and bugless. Of course, for that time span many new versions of Android itself was released with a bunch of changes. Perhaps because of that, the possibility exists that the app is not working anymore as it should. I don’t know. As it is still there, the chances are somebody is still using it, and that makes me feel pretty nice.
